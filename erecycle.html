Where do your electronics go?

It's not just paper, plastic and metal that can be recycled. Your electronics (small and large) have valuable components that can also be recycled. In addition, these electronics often have toxic substances that must be handled properly. It is your responsibility to make sure your electronics don't just end up in a landfill!


What kind of paper products can you recycle?
  • Computer & Copier Paper
  • Stationery
  • Newspaper and Magazines
  • Colored Paper
  • "Junk Mail"
  • Catalogs
  • Phone Books
  • Paperback Books
  • Hardback Books
  • Flat single-layer paperboard
  • Multi-layered, wavy-core corrugated cardboard
What kind of paper products can you not recycle?
  • Plastic-wrapped Paper
  • Loose Plastics
  • Food or Pizza Boxes
  • Tissues and Napkins
  • Paper Cups and Plates
  • Carbon Paper
  • Waxed Cardboard
  • Plastic Liners
  • Foam Peanuts
  • Polystyrene End Caps
What kind of bottles and cans can you recycle?
  • Clean Aluminum Foil
  • Laundry Detergent Jugs
  • Juice Boxes, Milk Cartons, and Aseptic Packaging
  • Cans, Bottles, Jugs and Jars made of glass, metal, or plastic # 1 through # 7
What kind of random stuff can you not recycle?
  • Caps and Lids
  • Glass Mirrors
  • China Plates or Cups
  • Light Bulbs
  • Window Pane Glass
  • Laboratory Glass
  • Metal items other than cans or foils
  • Plastic Shopping Bags
  • Films
  • Non-container plastics
