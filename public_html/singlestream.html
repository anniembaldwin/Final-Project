The magic of single stream recycling:
it all goes in the same bin!

What kind of paper products can you recycle?
  • Computer & Copier Paper
  • Stationery
  • Newspaper and Magazines
  • Colored Paper
  • "Junk Mail"
  • Catalogs
  • Pizza Boxes (with no major food residue)
  • Phone Books
  • Paperback Books
  • Hardback Books
  • Flat single-layer paperboard
  • Multi-layered, wavy-core corrugated cardboard
Tape, labels, and staples are ok!
What kind of paper products can you not recycle?
  • Plastic-wrapped Paper
  • Loose Plastics
  • Food
  • Tissues and Napkins
  • Paper Cups and Plates
  • Carbon Paper
  • Waxed Cardboard
  • Plastic Liners
  • Foam Peanuts
  • Polystyrene End Caps
What kind of bottles and cans can you recycle?
  • Clean Aluminum Foil
  • Laundry Detergent Jugs
  • Juice Boxes, Milk Cartons, and Aseptic Packaging
  • Cans, Bottles, Jugs and Jars made of glass, metal, or plastic # 1 through # 7
How do you recycle them?
Remove all food residue and rinse or drain any liquid.
What kind of random stuff can you not recycle?
  • Caps and Lids
  • Glass Mirrors
  • China Plates or Cups
  • Light Bulbs
  • Window Pane Glass
  • Laboratory Glass
  • Metal items other than cans or foils
  • Plastic Shopping Bags
  • Films
  • Non-container plastics
For more information, check out Harvard Recycling Guidelines
